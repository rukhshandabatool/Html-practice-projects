Main Ingredients

  1. 1 pound (450 g) Korean rice cakes (tteok, cylindrical type)
  2. 4-5 Korean fish cakes (optional, cut into strips)
  3. 3 cups water (or anchovy broth for richer flavor)
  4. 2 green onions (cut into 2-inch pieces)
  5. 1 boiled egg per serving (optional, for garnish)
  6. Sesame seeds (for garnish)

For the Sauce

  1. 3 tbsp gochujang (Korean red chili paste)
  2. 1 tbsp gochugaru (Korean chili flakes, optional for extra heat)
  3. 2 tbsp soy sauce
  4. 1 tbsp sugar (or honey)
  5. 1 tbsp corn syrup (or extra sugar)
  6. 2 tsp minced garlic
  7. 1 tsp sesame oil

  • If rice cakes are hard, soak them in warm water for 10-15 minutes.
  • In a wide pan, bring water (or broth) to a simmer.
  • Add gochujang, gochugaru, soy sauce, sugar, corn syrup, garlic, and sesame oil. Stir well to form the sauce.
  • Add rice cakes and cook on medium heat until soft and chewy (about 8-10 minutes), stirring occasionally.
  • Add fish cakes and green onions. Simmer for another 2-3 minutes until sauce thickens.
  • Top with boiled eggs and sesame seeds before serving.
