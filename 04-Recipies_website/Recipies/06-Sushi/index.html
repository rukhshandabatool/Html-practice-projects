For Sushi Rice

  • 2 cups sushi rice (short-grain Japanese rice)

  • 3 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 2 tbsp sugar

For Fillings (choose your favorites)

  1. 200 g fresh sushi-grade salmon or tuna (thinly sliced)
  2. 1 cucumber (cut into thin strips)
  3. 1 avocado (sliced)
  4. Carrot strips (optional, lightly blanched)
  5. Cooked shrimp or crab sticks (optional)

Other Essentials

  • 4-5 nori sheets (dried seaweed)
  • Soy sauce (for dipping)
  • Pickled ginger (for serving)
  • Wasabi paste (optional, for heat)
  • Bamboo sushi rolling mat (covered with cling film)

üë©‚Äçüç≥ Method

  1. Prepare the Rice: Wash the sushi rice several times until water runs clear. Cook with water (using a rice cooker or pot). Mix warm rice with rice vinegar, sugar, and salt. Let it cool.
  2. Prepare the Fillings: Slice fish, cucumber, avocado, and other fillings into thin strips.
  3. Set Up the Rolling Mat: Place a sheet of nori (shiny side down) on the bamboo mat.
  4. Spread the Rice: Wet your hands, then spread an even layer of rice over the nori, leaving 1 inch at the top edge free.
  5. Add Fillings: Place your chosen fillings (fish, cucumber, avocado, etc.) in a line across the center of the rice.
  6. Roll the Sushi: Lift the mat edge, roll tightly, and press gently to form a log. Seal the edge with a little water.
  7. Cut the Rolls: Slice with a sharp, wet knife into 6-8 pieces.
  8. Serve: Arrange on a plate. Serve with soy sauce, pickled ginger, and wasabi.
